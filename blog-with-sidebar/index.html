Melanie Martinez

by dine / on February 2, 2024

Crybaby Era

Melanie Martinez is an American singer/songwriter born in Astoria, Queens but was raised in Baldwin, New York. She first rose to fame after taking part on The Voice in 2012. After the show, she signed with Atlantic Records and released her first debut single “Dollhouse” with 584 million streams to this date. She later released her very first album “Crybaby” in 2015 with now over 400 million plays on YouTube.

K-12 Era

Taking a break from music, she went to work on her next album along with a movie titled “K-12” which she released in 2019. The movie shows how Crybaby (who is the character that Melanie plays throughout her career) and her friends fight against a controlling and morbid school. Following the K-12 album and movie, Melanie releases an album called “Afterschool” in 2020 to express her feelings when dealing with relationships and her overall feelings on certain topics as the songs have deep messages.
